  <p>This is based on the ancient <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Oxyrhynchus_hymn" target=_blank >Oxyrhynchus Hymn</a>, the earliest known manuscript of a Christian Greek hymn containing both lyrics and musical notation. The papyrus fragment bearing the hymn dates back to the late 3rd century AD.</p>
  <p>The hymn's discovery in Egypt is significant because the lyrics explicitly call out the Trinity long before the council of Nicea - wherein non-trinitarians like to claim the doctrine was invented. The hymn, however, proves that the Father, Son, and Holy Spirit were worshipped by Orthodox Christians long before the council ever convened.</p>
  <p>I have tried to stay as traditional as I can with this one. There is no metal here, and barely any instruments. I prompted Suno simply for traditional Byzantine chant that was reverent and worshipful. I did take a few liberties with arranging the structure and with the English translation - but nothing to take away any meaning from the hymn, and, importantly: nothing that any other composer of the era would see as an affront to the original.</p>
